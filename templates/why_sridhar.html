Sridhar Pabbisetty, age 34, MBA from IIM Bangalore, BE Computer Science from Bangalore University has resigned from his coveted post of Chief Operating Officer of the Centre for Public Policy, IIM Bangalore to contest for the Hebbal Assembly constituency in the Karnataka Assembly Elections (MLA) in April/May 2013.


Being actively involved in various initiatives to bring transparency and accountability in governance over the last five years, Sridhar understood well the task on hand and how challenges could be overcome by taking action instead of just complaining about every aspect of the city administration.

He chose to lead from the front and decided to start a movement which would encourage citizens to participate in both the creation and execution of policies that will improve the efficiency of the government, improve the safety of citizens, promote gender equality and help eradicate various forms of bribery prevalent in obtaining services from the government.

Sridhar Pabbisetty found all and more of his agenda in Lok Satta Party formed by the highly accomplished IAS officer Dr. Jayaprakash Narayan (refer Dr.JP and Lok Satta credentials and accomplishments in the following paragraphs). The Lok Satta Party is happy to nominate Sridhar Pabbisetty as their MLA candidate from Hebbal Constituency.


Credentials and Experience

Sridhar Pabbisetty has been working on ‘Inclusive Growth and Improvement of Governance’ in India for the last 5 years.

He was the coordinator of Karnataka Election Watch for the 2008 assembly and 2009 Lok Sabha elections and his efforts led to create awareness among the electorate about the Economic and Criminal antecedents of Candidates contesting for elections.

As the Enterprise Development Specialist at CHF International, he worked on improving the livelihoods and welfare of the Informal Sector Workers and Decentralized Solid Waste Management.

He has supported interventions to make banking accessible to the informal workers, improve skill training programs and to make social enterprises sustainable.

As the COO of Centre for Public Policy at IIMB, Sridhar has worked on advancing the research in the areas of inclusive education, enhancing employability and taught various elected representatives including MLAs and Local Body members as well as various civil servants on ensuring ‘Transparent and Accountability in Governance’ and bring in a culture of innovation and continuous improvement in their processes.

Sridhar’s focus has been on building and maintaining relationship with all members in the public policy ecosystem - government, regulatory bodies, industry, opinion leaders, researchers, partner institutions and foundations that are focused on policy research.

Sridhar Pabbisetty is a regular speaker at various national and international fora on the issue of ‘Open Governance and Inclusive Growth’.

He has been advising Government of Karnataka's Sakala Mission on ensuring timely delivery of services delivered the state. There are 265 services under Sakala and over 1.8 Crore service requests have been delivered so far. His continuous assessments and reports have enabled the mission to fix problems that exist in the delivery of these services and undertake continuous improvements of the processes involved in delivery of the services. Two of the services are being reengineered based on his recommendations - Conversion of Agricultural Land for non Agricultural purpose and Registration of schools and will take much less time and lesser number of documents.

Sridhar has been advising BESCOM adopting Open Governance. BESCOM now has a robust practice of voluntary disclosure of data and they can be accessed here -http:// bescom.org/en/4-i-a-2/ and http://bescom.org/en/rti-2/. As part of the Open Governance initiative BESCOM has now begun a Business Process Reengineering exercise involving customers who come to get their grievances become partners in simplifying the process BESCOM follows internally to service their requests.

Sridhar specializes in nurturing inclusive enterprises and helps them to develop long term corporate strategy and build business models with focus on scalability and sustainability.

Earlier he has worked with fast growing hi-tech companies such as ‘Zyme Solutions’ and ‘Aditi Technologies’.

Sridhar is a keen observer of Indian Politics and has been the ‘Coordinator of Karnataka Election Watch (KEW)’ for the Lok Sabha elections in 2009 as well as ‘Member of the KEW’ in Karnataka Assembly Elections in 2008.

Sridhar is also on the Board of ‘NammaCycle’ and ‘Reap Benefit’ and on the panel of various startup competitions including the ‘Acara Challenge’ and the ‘William James Foundation's Entrepreneurship Challenge’ focusing on a sustainable and equitable tomorrow.
